Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish featuring layers of wide, flat pasta sheets, a rich meat sauce, and creamy cheeses. The meat sauce, typically made from ground beef or sausage, is simmered with tomatoes, onions, garlic, and Italian herbs. This sauce is layered between tender lasagna noodles along with ricotta or cottage cheese and shredded mozzarella. The dish is topped with mozzarella and Parmesan, creating a golden-brown, bubbly crust as it bakes.

As it cooks, the flavors meld together, with the noodles soaking up the savory sauce and the cheeses melting into a creamy, stretchy texture. Each slice reveals distinct layers of pasta, sauce, and cheese, creating a satisfying combination of savory, tangy, and cheesy flavors. Lasagna is often served with garlic bread or a fresh salad for a complete and comforting meal.

Ingredients

  • Meat
  • Onion and garlic
  • Tomato products
  • Sugar
  • Spices and seasonings
  • Lasagna noodles
  • Cheeses
  • Egg

Steps

  1. Make the meat sauce.
  2. Cook the noodles.
  3. Make the ricotta mixture
  4. Layer the lasagna according to the recipe instructions.
  5. Cover with foil and bake.
  6. Let the lasagna rest before serving.
